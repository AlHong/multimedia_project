Uses of IMAX

  • Film production
    • IMAX cameras can record in high resolution and in different conditions (e.g. space, bottom of the ocean)
    • The cameras are commonly used for recording action scenes
    • A filmmaker who works with IMAX decides which parts should be shot with an IMAX camera, what techniques make the most of the IMAX screen, sound and theatre, and how to take advantage of IMAX’s format, color, scale and impact
    • 2D to 3D conversion
      • IMAX 3D conversion technology can turn films originally shot in 2D to be converted into 3D
  • Post production
    • IMAX Post/DKP is a post-production image and quality control facility.
  • Education
    • Some museums and educational institutions have IMAX theatres to show IMAX documentaries to educate and entertain more effectively.
  • film remasters
    • Most IMAX films are remastered into HDTV format for the MOJO HD, HDNet, and HD Theatre networks and releases on DVD and Blu-ray.
  • IMAX theatres: used for other than showing movies
    • The BFI IMAX Cinema in London is the first IMAX theatre to host live music concerts, by using a digital non-IMAX projector.
    • The Science Museum London and BFI IMAX Cinema have hosted electronic sports tournaments using digital projectors
  • Amusement parks
    • Use IMAX film in their rides
    • Example rides: Back to the Future: The Ride (formerly at Universal Studios), Horizons (formerly at EPCOT Center), Soarin’ Over California (Disney California Adventure Park & Epcot)
